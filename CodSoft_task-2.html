Tribute to Dr. Rajendra Prasad

The first President of India and a visionary leader

About Dr. Rajendra Prasad

Dr. Rajendra Prasad was an Indian independence activist, lawyer, scholar, and the first President of India, serving from 1950 to 1962. A true patriot and a leader with integrity, he played a pivotal role in shaping modern India.
On December 3, 2024, Lok Sabha Speaker Shri Om Birla led a solemn tribute to Dr. Rajendra Prasad, the first President of India, on his birth anniversary. The ceremony took place in the Central Hall of Samvidhan Sadan, where dignitaries gathered to honor the legacy of a leader who played a pivotal role in India’s history. This event not only commemorated Dr. Prasad’s contributions but also reminded attendees of the values he embodied.
Dr. Rajendra Prasad’s profound wisdom and steadfast leadership during pivotal moments in our history are a source of great pride. His endeavours as a champion of democracy and unity continue to resonate across generations. Tributes to him on his birth anniversary.

Notable Achievements

  • First President of India (1950-1962).
  • Played a key role in the Indian freedom movement under Mahatma Gandhi.
  • President of the Indian National Congress in 1934 and 1935.
  • Authored several books, including "India Divided" and his autobiography.
  • Awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1962.

Legacy

In 1915, Prasad appeared in the examination of masters in law from the Department of Law, University of Calcutta, passed the examination and won a gold medal. He completed his Doctorate in Law from Allahabad University. In 1916, he joined the High Court of Bihar and Odisha. In 1917, he was appointed as one of the first members of the Senate and of the Patna University. He also practised law at Bhagalpur, the famous silk town in Bihar.
Dr. Rajendra Prasad remains an inspiration to generations of Indians for his selfless service, humility, and dedication to the nation. His legacy continues to guide India towards a brighter future.
